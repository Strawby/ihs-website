A Historic Geological Landmark

Long before settlers reached Soda Creek, the Ute and Arapaho nations considered the hot springs a sacred healing ground. In 1863, the land was purchased and the first baths were built by George A. Jackson — with geological activity keeping the water hot ever since. As a preserved historic site more than 150-years-old, we appreciate your respect for the quirks of our building as we renovate and preserve for the future.
